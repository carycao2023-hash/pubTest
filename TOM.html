01

TOM Overview & Strategic Context

This Target Operating Model (TOM) defines how the AI Centre of Excellence (AI COE) will operate as the Group's global centralised AI delivery and governance engine. It translates the COE Charter mandate into a concrete organisational design, delivery mechanism, and operating rhythm.

🎯
TOM Design Intent

The TOM is designed to answer one primary question: Given a growing portfolio of identified AI use cases across two regions, how do we design, build, reuse, and uniformly manage these use cases at speed and scale — while establishing the governance discipline the Group currently lacks?

1.1 Key Design Drivers

Driver 1
⚡ Speed to Value via Use Case Portfolio

Multiple AI use cases already identified. The TOM must enable rapid sequencing, parallel execution, and component reuse — not slow down delivery with process overhead.

Driver 2
🏛️ Governance Without Bureaucracy

HK Region has the highest AI maturity but lacks unified governance. The TOM must introduce governance as an enabler, not a gate-keeper — lightweight enough to accelerate, robust enough to satisfy regulators.

Driver 3
🌐 Global Standards, Local Execution

A Global+Local model: COE owns the standards framework; regions own execution. HK Region is the pilot market where the model is first proven, then propagated to SG Region and beyond.

Driver 4
🗣️ Language-Aware Delivery

HK Region operates predominantly in Chinese (Traditional). The TOM must structurally account for language — in tooling, documentation, regulatory engagement, and talent — to avoid localisation becoming a chronic bottleneck.

1.2 Current State vs. Target State

COE Positioning — Global Centralised Delivery Layer

📌
Positioning Statement

The AI COE is the single global accountable entity for AI delivery across the Group. It owns the full AI product lifecycle — from demand intake and solution design through to build, deploy, and continuous operations. Business units and Digital Teams remain the demand originators and product owners; the COE is the delivery and capability engine that serves them.

2.1 What "Centralised Delivery" Means in Practice

Centralised
📐 Standards & Architecture

One set of AI development standards, platform architecture, and security/governance frameworks that apply globally. No regional variants unless explicitly carved out.

Centralised
🔧 Platform & Toolchain

Single shared AI/ML platform (MLOps + LLMOps), model registry, feature store, and monitoring stack — multi-region deployed but centrally managed by COE.

Centralised
👥 Talent Pool & Allocation

AI talent (Data Scientists, ML Engineers, AI Architects) sits in the COE globally. Allocation to use cases is managed centrally via a capacity and demand planning process.

Localised
🏙️ Demand & Product Ownership

Business requirements, prioritisation decisions, and product ownership remain with regional business and Digital Teams. They are the "clients" of the COE.

Localised
⚖️ Regulatory & Language Adaptation

Compliance interpretation, regulatory engagement, and language-specific artefacts are handled locally within COE's regional embedded function — not centralised away.

Given HK Region's unique combination of highest AI maturity + absent governance + Chinese-language operations + distinct regulatory environment (HKMA, SFC, IA), the embedded function is not merely a liaison role. It is a mini-COE nucleus that:

  • Acts as the primary interface between Global COE and HK/TW/MO business teams, operating in Traditional Chinese

  • Leads regulatory engagement with HKMA, SFC, and Insurance Authority

  • Serves as the governance pilot site — proving the Global+Local model before SG Region rollout

4.2 Stage Detail — Run & Optimise (Stage 5)

♾️
Why Ongoing Ops is a COE Responsibility

In the previous project model, models were "handed off" post go-live with no structured ownership, leading to silent model degradation, undetected drift, and ungoverned retraining. Under the TOM, COE retains ongoing operational accountability for all production AI models: monitoring, drift alerts, periodic retraining, performance reviews, and retirement decisions. This is operationalised through India Hub's MLOps team using the centralised platform.

Use Case Factory Model

The Use Case Factory is the COE's core operating mechanism for rapidly and consistently executing the identified AI use case portfolio. It treats use case delivery as a repeatable industrial process — not a series of unique projects — enabling velocity, quality, and reuse at scale.

Highlighted stages are where the Factory model creates the greatest speed advantage: standardised intake reduces assessment time; component assembly avoids rebuild; automated deployment removes manual steps.

5.1 Shared Component Library (AI Asset Catalogue)

The Factory depends on a well-maintained catalogue of reusable AI components. The COE owns and governs this catalogue globally; all regions contribute to and draw from it.

  • Reusable feature engineering pipelines
  • Synthetic data generators (for testing)
  • Data quality validation modules
Layer 2 · Model
Model & Algorithm Assets
  • Pre-trained base models (insurance domain fine-tuned)
  • Validated ML algorithm templates by use case type
  • LLM prompt libraries (EN + ZH)
  • Model evaluation & bias testing suites
Layer 3 · Application
Application & Integration Assets
  • API wrappers for core insurance platforms
  • Standard AI microservice templates
  • Explainability & output formatting components
  • Monitoring & alerting templates

5.2 Use Case Tiers & Fast-Track Lanes

Not all use cases are equal. The Factory operates three lanes to match delivery speed with risk profile:

LaneCriteriaTarget Time-to-ProductionGate RequirementsHK Relevance
🟢 Fast Lane Low risk, high asset reuse (≥70% existing components), internal tool 6–10 weeks Streamlined checklist; no full AIA Internal analytics, reporting automation
🟡 Standard Lane Medium risk, partial reuse, customer-facing or operational decision support 12–20 weeks Standard AIA, Tech Review, UAT Claims triage, sales recommendation
🔴 Complex Lane High risk, novel architecture, regulatory-sensitive, underwriting/claims decision automation 24–36 weeks Full AIA + Ethics Board + Regulator pre-notification Automated underwriting, fraud detection with enforcement action

HK Region Use Case Portfolio Acceleration

HK Region enters with the highest AI maturity — meaning many use cases are partially built or piloted but ungoverned. The Factory model addresses this by introducing a "Retroactive Governance" sprint for existing HK use cases:

  • Inventory & triage: catalogue all existing HK AI systems within 60 days of COE launch
  • Risk-tier all existing systems: apply standard AIA retrospectively to identify gaps
  • Fast-track compliant systems: those already meeting standards enter Run & Optimise immediately
  • Remediation backlog: non-compliant systems assigned to remediation sprints before new use cases are prioritised
  • This approach avoids blocking new use case delivery while systematically closing governance gaps

Location Strategy — China Innovation Hub & India Scale Hub

🗺️
Strategic Rationale

The COE's location strategy exploits the distinct talent and ecosystem strengths of two markets: China for high-calibre AI research talent, proximity to HK Region business, and deep domain innovation capability; India for large-scale, cost-efficient engineering delivery, strong MLOps & data engineering capability, and proven global delivery model maturity.

🇨🇳
China — Innovation Hub
Conceptualisation & Innovation
  • Primary focus: AI research, architecture design, PoC development, GenAI innovation
  • Key talent: AI Architects, Research Scientists, Senior Data Scientists, Solution Designers
  • HK Region proximity: same time zone, cultural proximity, Mandarin/Cantonese bilingual — natural support for HK use case design phase
  • Deliverables owned: Solution architecture, algorithm selection, PoC build, innovation pipeline, HK-specific design artefacts (ZH)
  • Key differentiator: Access to China's deep AI talent pool (top universities: Tsinghua, Peking, SJTU) and frontier model ecosystem
  • Governance note: China Hub operates under COE global standards; data handling must comply with PIPL and cross-border data rules; no HK/TW/MO customer data processed in China without explicit approval
🇮🇳
India — Scale Delivery Hub
Scale Execution & Operations
  • Primary focus: Use case build-at-scale, MLOps, data engineering, model monitoring & Ops, platform management
  • Key talent: ML Engineers, Data Engineers, DevOps/MLOps, QA, AI PMs
  • Scale advantage: Large English-proficient talent pool, cost-efficient for high-volume delivery, experienced in agile global delivery models
  • Deliverables owned: Production model development, platform CI/CD, data pipelines, ongoing model monitoring, test automation
  • Key differentiator: Proven insurance BPO/tech delivery ecosystem; multiple global insurers already operate AI delivery from India
  • Language note: India Hub operates in English; ZH-specific deliverables for HK Region are designed in China Hub and passed to India Hub for engineering execution with ZH specs

⏱️
Time Zone Management

China (UTC+8) and HK (UTC+8) are fully aligned. India (UTC+5:30) has a 2.5-hour overlap with HK's morning window. For HK-priority use cases, India Hub delivery squads should be structured for an early-morning start to maximise the synchronous window with HK. The COE Ops cadence and stand-ups should be scheduled to accommodate the China-India handoff window (09:00–11:30 IST / 11:30–14:00 HKT).

HK Region Deep-Dive

The HK Region (covering China Hong Kong, China Taiwan, and China Macao) represents the COE's highest-priority and most complex operating environment. It is simultaneously the Group's most AI-mature market and the one most in need of governance formalisation. It is also the designated pilot region for proving the Global+Local operating model.

7.1 HK Region Profile

Maturity Assessment
🏆 Highest AI Maturity
  • Most AI use cases already deployed or piloted
  • Established AI/Data talent base in region
  • Strong appetite from business for AI expansion
  • Gap: ungoverned — no unified standards, model documentation, or risk oversight in place
Key Challenges
⚠️ Operating Complexity
  • Multi-jurisdiction: HKMA + SFC + IA (HK) + FSC (TW) + AMCM (MO)
  • Chinese-language dominant operations; English-only standards = friction
  • No China mainland business (policy constraint) — data flows within HK/TW/MO only
  • Legacy AI systems with unknown risk profiles need retroactive assessment

7.2 HK Region Operating Model

HK Region Embedded Function — Team Composition

The HK Embedded Function is the COE's local delivery nerve centre. It is a small but high-impact team designed to bridge global standards with local execution:

  • HK AI Lead (1 FTE): Senior AI leader, bilingual (EN/ZH-Traditional), reports to Head of AI COE. Primary interface to HK Digital Team and business. Accountable for all HK use case delivery outcomes.
  • Bilingual AI Business Analyst / Solution Designer (1–2 FTE): Translates business requirements (ZH) into structured AI design briefs (bilingual EN+ZH) for China Hub. Critical role in preventing requirements-loss-in-translation.
  • HK Governance & Compliance Specialist (1 FTE): Deep knowledge of HKMA, SFC, IA regulations as they apply to AI. Owns local regulatory calendar, pre-engagement with regulators, and audit readiness.
  • Taiwan / Macao Liaisons (0.5 FTE each or shared): Part-time embedded contacts in TW and MO, primarily for regulatory and business engagement; leverages HK lead for AI governance.

Global + Local Governance & Standards Framework

The COE operates a two-tier standards architecture: a non-negotiable Global Core Layer that applies everywhere, and a Local Adaptation Layer where regions can extend (never weaken) the global baseline to meet local regulatory and operational requirements.

Global Core Layer — Non-Negotiable
🌐 Group-Wide Standards
  • AI Risk Classification Framework (3 tiers)
  • AI Impact Assessment (AIA) methodology
  • Model documentation standard (Model Card)
  • Responsible AI principles (fairness, explainability, privacy)
  • AI development lifecycle (SDLC) with Gate checkpoints
  • Platform & toolchain requirements
  • Data governance minimum standards
  • Language: English (authoritative version)
Local Adaptation Layer — HK Region
🏙️ HK-Specific Extensions
  • HKMA / IA / SFC regulatory mapping overlay
  • ZH-Traditional versions of all process artefacts
  • PDPO (HK) data handling addendum
  • TW FSC compliance addendum
  • MO AMCM compliance addendum
  • ZH-first model cards for HK-deployed systems
  • Local regulatory engagement protocol
  • Language: Traditional Chinese (primary), English (reference)
🔑
Design Rule: Local Can Extend, Never Weaken

The Local Adaptation Layer may add stricter requirements above the Global Core (e.g., HK requiring bilingual model cards, or TW requiring additional regulatory pre-approval steps). It may never reduce or bypass Global Core requirements. This is the enforcement mechanism that gives the Group governance consistency while allowing regional compliance.

8.1 HK as Governance Pilot — Propagation to Other Regions

HK Region is intentionally chosen as the governance pilot because of its AI maturity, making rapid iteration possible. The learnings from HK's Global+Local implementation will be packaged into a "Region Activation Kit" for subsequent rollout to SG Region and other markets:

Kit Component 1
📦 Standards Package

Localised standard templates (adapted from HK model), regulatory mapping worksheet, and compliance checklist for new regions to complete

Kit Component 2
🎓 Onboarding Programme

Training curriculum for regional embedded functions, including governance processes, platform onboarding, and language/localisation protocols

Kit Component 3
📊 Governance Dashboard

Pre-built region-level governance dashboard (model inventory, risk status, AIA completion rate, regulatory calendar) deployable within 2 weeks

Language & Localisation Strategy

Language is a structural operating risk for the HK Region, not a cosmetic issue. A COE operating primarily in English, serving a business environment that operates in Traditional Chinese, will systematically lose speed and quality at every requirements-gathering, documentation, and regulatory-engagement touchpoint. The TOM addresses this structurally.

⚠️
Risk: Language as a Delivery Bottleneck

Without deliberate structural design, language barriers will manifest as: requirements mistranslation; bilingual team members becoming informal translators (reducing their productive capacity); regulatory submissions that fail on language grounds; and HK business users disengaging from AI systems they don't understand. These are not hypothetical — they are the observed failure modes in comparable global COE deployments.

9.1 Bilingual Operating Model for HK Region

🇬🇧
English — Global COE Standard
  • All Global Core standards and policies (authoritative)
  • India Hub deliverables (code, technical docs)
  • COE-wide communications and governance meetings
  • Cross-region reporting and dashboards
  • Vendor and cloud provider engagement
  • International regulatory submissions
🇭🇰
Traditional Chinese — HK Region Primary
  • Business requirements and demand intake (ZH forms)
  • Solution design briefs for HK use cases (bilingual EN+ZH)
  • User-facing AI system outputs and interfaces
  • HKMA / IA / SFC regulatory submissions and responses
  • HK model cards and compliance documentation
  • Training materials for HK business teams
  • HK Governance Dashboard (bilingual)

9.2 Language Operating Protocols

Protocol 1
ZH-First Requirements

HK business teams submit AI demand intake in Traditional Chinese. The HK Bilingual BA translates into a structured English brief before passing to COE Global / India Hub. This is a defined role, not ad-hoc translation.

Protocol 2
Bilingual Design Artefacts

For HK use cases, solution design documents (architecture, data flow, user journey) are produced in bilingual format (EN primary, ZH-Traditional alongside) by the China Hub with HK Embedded Function review.

Protocol 3
ZH Regulatory Submissions

HKMA, IA and other HK regulator submissions are produced in Traditional Chinese by the HK Governance Specialist. English summaries provided for COE Global governance records. Legal review conducted in ZH.

Protocol 4
LLM / GenAI Language Policy

For AI systems deployed in HK with customer-facing NLP components, Traditional Chinese must be a first-class supported language — not a translation of an English output. Model evaluation must include ZH-specific benchmark metrics (ROUGE-ZH, human eval in ZH).

Technology & Platform Architecture

The COE operates a single unified AI platform deployed in a multi-region topology. The platform is managed centrally by COE's India Hub MLOps team, with region-specific deployment nodes to address data sovereignty requirements.

Layer 1 · Experimentation
🔬 Dev & Experimentation
  • Unified notebook environment (Jupyter / VS Code)
  • Experiment tracking (MLflow / W&B)
  • Feature store (shared + region-partitioned)
  • Data catalogue with lineage
Layer 2 · Production
🏭 MLOps Pipeline
  • CI/CD for models (automated training + deploy)
  • Model registry (versioning + approval workflow)
  • Containerised serving (Kubernetes)
  • A/B testing & shadow mode
Layer 3 · Observability
📡 Model Monitoring
  • Real-time performance dashboards
  • Data & model drift detection
  • Automated retraining triggers
  • Explainability outputs (SHAP / LIME)

HK Region Platform Considerations

  • Data residency: HK Region deployment node hosted in Hong Kong or compliant GBA data centre — HK/TW/MO customer data does not traverse to India Hub or China Hub infrastructure without explicit legal basis
  • ZH-capable LLM endpoint: COE maintains a dedicated Traditional Chinese-capable LLM endpoint for HK Region use cases (fine-tuned or prompted on ZH-Traditional, not simplified Chinese)
  • HKMA cloud compliance: Cloud vendor selection and architecture must comply with HKMA's Supervisory Policy Manual on cloud computing; COE maintains the cloud vendor due diligence register for HK
  • Audit trail: HK platform node maintains local audit logs in compliance with HKMA audit trail requirements; logs accessible by HK Governance Specialist for regulatory inspection readiness

B. Open Questions for Stakeholder Resolution

❓
Items Requiring Executive Decision

The following design choices have been made with stated assumptions; they should be reviewed and confirmed by the COE Steering Committee at charter approval:

  1. China Hub data policy: Confirm that HK/TW/MO customer data cannot be processed in China Hub under any circumstance, or define the permissible data classification boundary. This affects use case design for HK.
  2. HK Embedded Function reporting line: Does HK AI Lead report to Head of AI COE (globally consistent) or to HK Regional CTO (stronger local accountability)? Recommendation is a dual reporting line.
  3. India Hub vendor model: Is India Hub an internal headcount build, a strategic outsourcing arrangement, or a hybrid? The TOM is designed to accommodate all three but the governance model differs.
  4. Language authority: In the event of conflict between English (global standard) and ZH (local adaptation) documentation, which is authoritative for regulatory purposes in HK? Recommendation: ZH-Traditional for local regulatory submissions; English for Group governance records.
  5. HK legacy system remediation timeline: Are there any politically sensitive HK AI systems where retroactive AIA may surface findings that require executive-level handling?
